Mr. Lee is currently a lawyer specializing in immigration and commercial law in Portland, Oregon and Seattle, Washington.

Prior to his current position as a law firm, Thomas was a lawyer in the US and the United States at large law firms (Lee & Ko, Garvey, Schubert & Barer), the United States Department of Commerce and the Washington Supreme Court.

He also worked as an intern at the United States Department of Justice and the US Trade Representative's Office of Justice at the Catholic University of Korea, where he lectured on American law and law.

He is the author of "The Story of Getting a US Visa for Nurses" (Gunja Publishing Co., 2009). He has also held seminars on US investment visas and international trade at the Seoul Bar Association, the Korea Chamber of Commerce and Industry, the COEX Trade Center in Seoul, the Portland Trade Center, the Nursing University, the Bank, etc. He has been a columnist for immigration law through various media. We wrote a column on immigration law and business law in Vancouver International Student, Christian Daily, and Korea Daily, Media Korea, Real Estate Bank, Crossroads, Living Senses.
